The Story

*As a co-founder, I wore many different hats, no matter how uncomfortable or unfamiliar - this case study will primarily focus on my experiences with motion as opposed to my UI/UX responsibilities.

I would argue that video’s primary function is rooted in entertainment. Flashy titles, rapid transitions and awe-inspiring graphics are mostly used to enhance fun around subject matter crafted for entertainment, as opposed to subject matter concerning education.

The challenge with educational videos is that educators are traditionally trained to perform in a person-to-person environment, which has numerous advantages over communicating through a screen, even with the help of assistive graphics, motion graphics and UI. When training motion designers who come from commercial motion design backgrounds, I have found that it is tremendously important for motion designers to focus on mixing just enough entertainment techniques to enhance commnication, rather than diminish it.
